Live data streaming using Pyspark; Sentiment Analysis on Tweets about NFL Teams

In this project, I used PySpark to stream live Twitter data related to all 32 NFL teams and performed real-time sentiment analysis to assess how fan sentiment correlates with each team's performance. Tweets were processed and scored for positive and negative sentiment, then aggregated and compared against each team's win percentage.

The analysis revealed an interesting trend: teams with lower win percentages exhibited more extreme sentiment, both positive and negative. Surprisingly, the distribution of tweets skewed more positive than expected. Histograms showed a left-skew in positive sentiment (indicating a higher volume of favorable tweets), while negative sentiment distributions were right-skewed, highlighting their relative scarcity. These findings challenge the common assumption that social media is overwhelmingly critical of underperforming teams and offer a more nuanced view of fan engagement in professional sports.
